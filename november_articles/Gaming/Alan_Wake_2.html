Alan Wake 2:
The First True Next-Gen
Alan Franklin

After over a decade, the sequel to the cult classic Alan Wake has finally arrived. Alan Wake 2 is a direct sequel to the original game and still connects to Remedy’s other titles like Control. The game follows two protagonists, detective Saga Anderson and writer Alan Wake. Saga Anderson is sent to solve a murder case in the city of the original game. The events that occur are events that are written out in a book by none other than Alan Wake. Alan Wake and Saga Anderson’s stories work in parallel to each other as Saga deals with everything happening in the real world while Alan fights his way through his own books. The storyline is packed with twists, unique mechanics, and intriguing enemy types that’ll keep you entertained throughout your playthrough.

Alan Wake 2 is a technological wonder that provides some of the best visuals in gaming. Alan Wake 2 uses Remedy’s Northlight engine to create lifelike visuals, animation, and physics without sacrificing too much performance. Alan Wake 2 is still really taxing on your PC, even high-end PCs will struggle to run the game at a high frame rate. The game’s recommended PC specs are generous, as those specs will barely be able to run the game.

Alan Wake 2 takes a different approach from its predecessor as it's more focused on survival horror. Remedy seems to have taken great inspiration from Capcom’s Resident Evil franchise. Limited items, dark environments, and the use of small groups of enemies that take several bullets to take down provide the game with its survival-horror feel made popular by Capcom.

Alan Wake 2 has received overwhelmingly positive reviews from several different sources. Creative writing, smooth gameplay, and remarkable visuals have allowed Alan Wake 2 to lead the charge for a game of the year.
