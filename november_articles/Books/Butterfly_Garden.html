The Butterfly Garden
Unclaimed

TW: Graphic descriptions, violence, self-harm and suicide, sexual content, abuse

I recently read a book called “The Butterfly Garden", and it easily turned into one of my favorite books. It was a suspenseful and gripping thriller that kept me on my feet. The book starts off with officers interrogating a survivor, Maya, one of the victims from the garden full of “butterflies”- young women who have been kidnapped and intricately tattooed to resemble the new names they were given. The officers have suspicion that she is somehow involved with the kidnappings since the other survivors wouldn't speak with anyone without talking to Maya first. Victor and Brandon are assigned to the task of making her talk and hear her side of the story. She slowly reveals stories of her tragic past and of horrific tales of a man who’d go to any length to hold beauty captive. The more she shares the more the agents have suspicions that she is hiding something.

The Butterfly Garden quickly became Dot Hutchisons’ most famous book with 4.1/5 stars on good reads, it was even nominated for best horror in 2016. The book gained so much attention that they mentioned it was to be turned into a movie, with the director being Oscar winner, Michael Sugar. I truly recommend this book to anyone who is a fan of thriller books and is looking for a good book to read.
