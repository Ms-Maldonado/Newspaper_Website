Silent Patient
Unclaimed

-Mystery and Thriller-

From beginning to end, "The Silent Patient" is a compelling psychological thriller that skillfully crafts suspense, and keeps readers guessing and at the edge of their seats from start to finish. This novel revolves around a talented painter who seemingly has it all, but remains mute after shooting her husband, Gabriel, in the face. Through a skillful and clever use of narrative technique, Alex Michaelides alternates between Alicia's diary entries and the perspective of her therapist, Theo Faber, who is determined to help her speak once more. As the story progresses, an intricate web of lies, psychological tension, and secrets are revealed to the reader. The author's background in psychotherapy shines through as he delves into the intricacies of the human mind, and the nature of trauma and obsession. The book has a great pace with short, snappy chapters that propel the plot. It's a page-turner that's hard to put down. The theme of the thin line separating sanity from madness is skillfully explored by the author, who also gives strong character development. The book is a gripping psychological thriller with many twists and turns, but the ending might not be to everyone's taste. This conclusion will probably provoke conversations and arguments among readers, making this a great book. All things considered, "The Silent Patient" is a gripping thriller that deeply inquires into the human psyche. Fans of the genre and anybody who appreciates a clever, perplexing mystery should read it.
